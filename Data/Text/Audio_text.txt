 So, what are large language models or LLMs? Large language models are neural networks with billions of parameters, trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human language. Some key characteristics include Massive scale. Large language models contain billions or even trillions of parameters. Extensive training. They are trained on diverse texts from books, websites, and documents. Emergent abilities. Large language models develop capabilities not explicitly programmed. Transfer learning. Large language models can be fine-tuned for specific tasks. Let's explore how large language models or LLMs work. The first step is tokenization. Text is broken down into tokens, which are words or subwords, and then converted to numerical representations. Second, embedding. Tokens are transformed into vectors in a high-dimensional space that capture semantic meaning. Third, processing. Transformer layers process the embeddings using self-attention to capture relationships between tokens. Fourth generation. The model predicts the next token based on previous context, building the response one token at a time. It's important to note that LLMs don't understand text in a human sense. They learn statistical patterns in language through massive parameter optimization during training. Large language models, or LLMs, undergo two main phases of training. The first phase is pre-training. In this phase the model learns language patterns from massive text corpora through self learning The objective is to predict the next token or mass tokens The data includes hundreds of billions of tokens from books websites and articles The compute power required involves thousands of GPUs or TPUs for weeks or even months. The second phase is fine-tuning. In this phase the pre-trained model is adapted for specific tasks or to align with human preferences. This This includes reinforcement learning from human feedback, or RLHF, instruction tuning, which involves teaching models to follow instructions and domain adaptation, specializing for specific fields. Let's take a look at some key large language model, or LLM, architectures. First, BERT by Google. BERT stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers. It focuses on understanding context by looking at words from both directions. Next, GPT by OpenAI. GPT stands for Generative Pre-trained Transformer. It specializes in generating coherent and contextually relevant text continuations. Next, T5 by Google. T5 stands for Text-to-Text Transfer Transformer. It frames all natural language processing, or NLP, tasks as text-to-text problems, providing a unified approach. Next, LAMA by Meta. LAMA stands for Large Language Model Meta AI. It is an open-source foundation model designed for research and commercial applications. Next, POM by Google. POM stands for Pathways Language Model. It uses a new architecture called Pathways to enable training on multiple tasks simultaneously. And finally, Clawed by Anthropic.
